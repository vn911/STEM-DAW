Pythagoras' Famous Works

  • Pythagorean Theorem: \( a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \)
  • Pythagorean Triples: Sets of three positive integers \( (a, b, c) \) that satisfy the theorem
  • Concept of Numbers: He proposed that numbers have intrinsic meanings and relationships.
  • Mathematics of Music: He discovered that musical intervals can be expressed in simple numerical ratios.
  • Geometry: Contributions to geometric properties and shapes.

Pythagoras: The Father of Numbers

Pythagoras was an ancient Greek philosopher and mathematician, best known for his contributions to mathematics, especially the Pythagorean Theorem, which states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides.

Born on the island of Samos around 570 BCE, Pythagoras traveled widely in search of knowledge and later founded a school in Croton, Italy. His school promoted a philosophy that combined mathematics, spirituality, and ethics. He and his followers believed that numbers were the essence of all things and that the universe was fundamentally ordered by mathematical principles.
